03 // RETURN CONCENTRATION

Why Most Yearly Returns Come in a Select Few Months

For directional strategies, annual PnL is rarely distributed evenly across 12 months. In most years, the majority of a strategy's return is delivered in a handful of high-conviction periods when the market regime aligns with its alpha signals.

STRUCTURAL FACTOR · REGIME DEPENDENCE
Directional strategies like OptimaShort make money when their directional bias matches the prevailing trend. Bear months produce cluster wins for short-biased strategies; bull months produce cluster wins for long-biased ones. In neutral or choppy months, signals are mixed and the engine typically prints small gains or small losses. The result: a year of monthly returns often looks like a few large positive prints surrounded by small noise.
STRUCTURAL FACTOR · SIGNAL THRESHOLD
The engine requires a minimum conviction threshold before opening a position. During low-volatility periods, fewer signals clear the threshold, so trading frequency drops. When volatility expands (as in early June), the engine's signal conviction rises sharply, position sizes scale up, and a small number of high-conviction trades can dominate monthly PnL.
PRACTICAL TAKEAWAY
Looking at any single strategy's monthly PnL curve, expect to see 2–4 months per year that account for 60–80% of the annual return. The other 8–10 months are typically flat-to-modestly-positive chop. This is by design — the engine is built to wait for its edge and deploy aggressively when the edge appears. Investors evaluating these strategies should measure performance over rolling 6–12 month windows, not month-by-month.
